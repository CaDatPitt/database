To browse the public datasets on the site, click the "Browse Datasets" link on the blue navigation bar at the top of the website. You can browse datasets without an account.

These datasets are commonly used by researchers who include data about library and archives collections in their research. Importantly, this data is metadata which describes an item, not the text/content of the item itself. For example, if a student was working on a project involving metadata and needed a source for metadata, these datasets could be that source. If a scholar researching a particular archival collection in the ULS and needs the metadata about those items, they could access that here and create a dataset with that collection. Anyone who is curious about library and archives collections can browse the public datasets to learn more.

Overall, the most common data type in these datasets is text. However, data types could vary depending upon the collection.

To create an account, click the blue and yellow "Sign Up" button in the navigation bar at the top of the page, to the left of the search bar. This will take you to a form, where you can enter your information to create an account.

First, log in to your account or sign up for an account if you do not have one already. Then, go to the Browse Datasets page and use the filters to select some datasets. Locate the button shaped like a pin, and click this to pin the dataset. Once a dataset is pinned, you will be able to view it on your Dashboard on the left side, under Pinned Datasets.

First, log in to your account or sign up for one if you do not have one yet. Then, go to the Browse Data page. For each dataset, there will be a gold button labeled “Download”. Click this, and a Download Dataset window will appear. Use the drop down menu to select a file type, then click the gold Submit button at the bottom of the window to download the dataset.

Currently, CSV(.csv) and Excel Workbook(.xlsx) files are available for download. If you have suggestions for data formats that could be added for download, please feel free to contact us.

First, you will need to create an account on the site, or log in to your account if you already have one. Then, use the "Retrieve Data" link on the blue navigaton bar. On this page, you can request data by selecting one or more collections or by listing unique identifiers associated with collection items, then clicking the "Retrieve" button. Once your initial results have been loaded, you can apply filters based on your criteria to narrow your results. When you're ready to create a dataset, click the "Create Dataset" button and fill out the form with the required information about your dataset. All of the items in your results will be automatically added to your dataset. Once you have created a dataset, you can edit the dataset information, add and remove items, and view your dataset on your Dashboard in the first tab, "My Datasets".

To view identifiers, go to the Browse Datasets page and click on the title of the dataset to view the items in that collection. For each item, underneath the item title, there is the identifier which begins with “pitt:” followed by numbers.

First, log in to your account or sign up for one if you do not have one yet. Then, go to your Dashboard page by clicking the “Hi, [your name]” link on the navigation bar and choosing Dashboard on the drop down menu. On the left side of this page, choose the My Datasets tab. Locate the dataset you would like to change. To delete a dataset, click the trash can button and click the red delete button in the pop up window. To edit a dataset, click the pencil button and choose edit a copy to keep the current dataset and create a copy to edit, or original to edit the current dataset.

The terms of use vary upon the organization who owns the dataset. To view this information, see the terms of use for that specific dataset.

The terms of use for datasets published on CaDatPittDB follow the CC BY 4.0 License.
